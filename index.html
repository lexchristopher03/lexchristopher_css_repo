About the Colorado Symphony

The Colorado Symphony Orchestra (CSO) is a professional orchestra based in Denver, Colorado. Founded in 1900, it is known for its vibrant performances and diverse repertoire, which ranges from classical masterpieces to contemporary works and popular genres. The orchestra performs at the Boettcher Concert Hall in Denver and collaborates with renowned guest artists, conductors, and composers. The CSO aims to engage and inspire audiences through innovative programming, educational initiatives, and community outreach. It features a range of concerts, including classical series, pops concerts, and family-friendly performances. The orchestra also emphasizes inclusivity and accessibility, striving to reach a broad audience and foster a love for orchestral music in the community.
